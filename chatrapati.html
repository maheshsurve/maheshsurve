Early life

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj was born on 19 February 1630 at Shivneri, a hill fort located near Junnar town in Pune district.
[8] The exact date of birth of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj is a matter of disagreement among historians.
The Maharashtra state government accepted the birth date of Lord Shiva in 2001 as Falgun Vadya Tritiya Shaka 1551 (Friday, 19 February 1630).
[9] Among other possible dates, April 6, 1627 (Vaishakh Shuddh Tritiya) was considered a date of birth. [10]
The Government of Maharashtra has listed 19 February as a holiday to commemorate the birth anniversary of Shivaji Maharaj.

Background And References

In 1636, the Adil Royal Sultanate of Bijapur invaded the southern states. [30] The sultanate had recently become a kingdom of the Mughal Empire.

Shahaji raje was then a chieftain of the hilly regions of western India and was helping adilshahi. Shahajiraje was looking for opportunities for the reward of Jahangir in the conquered region, on which he could collect annual taxes.

Conflict And Bijapur Sultanate

In 1646, taking advantage of the chaos created in the Bijapur court due to the illness of the Sultan, 16-year-old Shivaji took the Torna fort and seized the large treasure found there. [38] [39] Over the next two years, Shivaji took several important forts near Pune.
These include Purandhar, Kondhana and Chakan. They also took direct control of Supe, Baramati and Indapur. They conquered the mountain of Murumbadev in front of Torangad and repaired it and named it Rajgad. [40] For this, they used the treasure found at Torana. Rajgarh was their capital for more than a decade.
